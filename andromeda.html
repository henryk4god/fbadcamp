Introduction

This guide is designed for beginners and digital product developers looking to launch highly effective paid advertising campaigns on Meta (Facebook/Instagram) following the latest "Andromeda Update."

Important: Strategy Shift

This new strategy shifts focus away from complex audience targeting and places the emphasis squarely on creative testing and utilizing Meta's advanced optimization capabilities.

Phase 1

The Campaign Level – Setting the Foundation (Budget)

The Campaign level is where you define your advertising objective and, critically, how your budget will be managed.

Step 1

Choose Your Objective

When you start a new campaign, the first step is to select what you want people to do. For launching a digital product, the recommended choice is the Sales objective.

This tells Meta's algorithm to prioritize finding people most likely to purchase your product.

Step 2

Name Your Campaign

Give your campaign a clear, descriptive name.

Naming Example:

Sales_Product_Launch_Q4_CBO
Step 3

Implement Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO)

This is the most critical change in the Andromeda strategy. You must choose Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO).

What this means:

Instead of setting a budget for each audience group (Ad Set), you set one budget for the entire campaign. Meta's system will automatically distribute this budget to the winning ad creatives, ensuring your money is spent on the best opportunities.

Action:

Find the budget section and select CBO.

Step 4

Set Your Daily Budget

Input the amount you are prepared to spend daily on this specific campaign. This daily budget will be distributed across all the ads you create within this campaign.

Phase 2

The Ad Set Level – Defining Where and Who (Broad)

The Ad Set level determines the conversion destination, schedule, and primary audience parameters. Crucially, you will only be using ONE Ad Set.

Step 1

Define Your Conversion Location and Goal

This specifies where you want your prospects to go after clicking your ad.

Website

Select if you are sending traffic to a landing page or e-commerce store.

Message Destination

Select if you want direct messages (e.g., WhatsApp, Messenger).

Conversion Goal:

For a website, set your performance goal to Maximize Number of Conversions.

Step 2

Integrate Your Data Set (Pixel)

You must select and ensure your Meta Pixel (now often called the Data Set) is correctly installed on your website.

This is how Meta tracks purchases or leads to optimize your campaign.

Step 3

Set Your Schedule

Set an end date for your ad. It is highly recommended that you allow your ad to run for a minimum of 5 days, and ideally a full week, to give Meta enough time to gather data and optimize performance.

Step 4

Use Broad Audience Targeting

Under the Andromeda strategy, complex Detailed Targeting (using interests) is often unnecessary and can hurt performance. The new system is smart enough to find the right buyers without heavy targeting.

Focus only on these core parameters:

Location

Specify your target market. For digital products, you can target multiple Cities (up to 20 maximum) or countries.

Age

Set a relevant minimum and maximum age range (e.g., 25–55) based on your ideal customer profile.

Gender

Leave this set to All unless your product is strictly gender-specific.

Avoid detailed interest targeting - Let Meta's AI find your buyers based on creative performance.

Step 5

Optimize Placements

Define where your ads will appear (Facebook, Instagram, etc.).

Devices

For many markets, mobile traffic is dominant. You may choose to remove Desktop placements and focus only on Mobile.

Platforms

Focus your budget. It is best practice to remove:

  • Threads
  • Audience Network
  • Messenger
