## ðŸŽ¯ Phase 1: The Campaign Level â€“ Setting the Foundation (Budget)

The Campaign level is where you define your advertising objective and, critically, how your budget will be managed.

### **Step 1: Choose Your Objective**
When you start a new campaign, the first step is to select what you want people to do. For launching a digital product, the **recommended choice is the Sales objective**. This tells Meta's algorithm to prioritize finding people most likely to purchase your product.

### **Step 2: Name Your Campaign**
Give your campaign a clear, descriptive name.  
**Example:** `Sales_Product_Launch_Q4_CBO`

### **Step 3: Implement Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO)**
This is the **most critical change** in the Andromeda strategy. You must choose **Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO)**.

**What this means:** Instead of setting a budget for each audience group (Ad Set), you set **one budget for the entire campaign**. Meta's system will automatically distribute this budget to the winning ad creatives, ensuring your money is spent on the best opportunities.

**Action:** Find the budget section and select **CBO**.

### **Step 4: Set Your Daily Budget**
Input the amount you are prepared to spend **daily** on this specific campaign. This daily budget will be distributed across all the ads you create within this campaign.

## ðŸŽ¯ Phase 2: The Ad Set Level â€“ Defining Where and Who (Broad)

The Ad Set level determines the **conversion destination, schedule, and primary audience parameters**.  
Crucially, you will only be using **ONE Ad Set**.

### **Step 1: Define Your Conversion Location and Goal**
This specifies where you want your prospects to go after clicking your ad.

**Location:**
- Select **Website** if you are sending traffic to a landing page or e-commerce store.
- If you want direct messages, select **Message Destination** (e.g., WhatsApp, Messenger).

**Conversion Goal:** For a website, set your performance goal to **Maximize Number of Conversions**.

### **Step 2: Integrate Your Data Set (Pixel)**
You must select and ensure your **Meta Pixel** (now often called the **Data Set**) is correctly installed on your website. This is how Meta tracks purchases or leads to optimize your campaign.

### **Step 3: Set Your Schedule**
Set an end date for your ad. It is highly recommended that you allow your ad to run for a **minimum of 5 days**, and ideally a full week, to give Meta enough time to gather data and optimize performance.

### **Step 4: Use Broad Audience Targeting**
Under the Andromeda strategy, complex Detailed Targeting (using interests) is often unnecessary and can hurt performance. The new system is smart enough to find the right buyers without heavy targeting.

Focus **only** on these core parameters:
- **Location:** Specify your target market. For digital products, you can target multiple **Cities (up to 20 maximum)** or countries.
- **Age:** Set a relevant minimum and maximum age range (e.g., 25â€“55) based on your ideal customer profile.
- **Gender:** Leave this set to **All** unless your product is strictly gender-specific.

### **Step 5: Optimize Placements**
Define where your ads will appear (Facebook, Instagram, etc.).

**Devices:** For many markets, mobile traffic is dominant. You may choose to remove **Desktop** placements and focus only on **Mobile**.

**Platforms:** Focus your budget. It is best practice to remove:
- Threads
- Audience Network
- Messenger

And concentrate your spend on **Facebook and Instagram feeds**.

## ðŸŽ¯ Phase 3: The Ad Level â€“ Creative Testing (The Key Focus)

This is the **most labor-intensive but critical phase**. The Andromeda Update is banking more on ads than ad sets, meaning the quality and variety of your creative material are what will drive success.

### **Step 1: Create Multiple Test Ads**
You need to create **3 to 5 separate ads** within your single Ad Set. You can achieve this by:
- Using the dedicated **"Setup Test"** feature in the Ads Manager, which will auto-duplicate the ad.
- Manually duplicating your initial ad (using the **'Duplicate'** option) 3-5 times.

### **Step 2: Define Your Test Comparison**
Since you are running a sales campaign, you should tell the system to compare your ads based on **Cost Per Purchase**. This ensures Meta prioritizes the ads that get you the cheapest results.

### **Step 3: Design Unique Marketing Angles for Each Ad**
For each of your 3 to 5 duplicated ads, you must use:
- A **different image or video creative**
- A **unique selling point (marketing angle)** in the ad copy

#### **The Concept:**
People buy the same digital product for different reasons. Your ads must appeal to these varied motivations.

#### **Example Marketing Angles (If selling a course on Facebook Ads):**

| Ad | Marketing Angle | Target Customer Mindset |
|----|-----------------|--------------------------|
| **Ad #1** | **The Income Angle:** Focus on using the product as a side hustle or second source of income. | *"I need to make more money."* |
| **Ad #2** | **The Business Growth Angle:** Focus on using the product to sell their own services or grow an existing business. | *"I need to scale my company."* |
| **Ad #3** | **The Career Angle:** Focus on enhancing skills to get a better job or earn a promotion in the current workplace. | *"I need to improve my resume."* |

**Repeat this process** for all 3 to 5 ads, ensuring each one tackles a different fear, addresses a different aspiration, or uses a different visual hook.

### **Step 4: Launch and Optimize**
Once your campaign, single Ad Set, and multiple unique Ads are set up, publish them all.

The **CBO** feature will automatically begin testing the different ads with your daily budget.
